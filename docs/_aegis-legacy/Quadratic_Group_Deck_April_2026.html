2. The Vision & Corporate Structure
What does Bloomberg + Duolingo + Palantir look like if you built them from the same data layer?

Educate

Aegis Empire (under Agon) teaches finance through simulation — real market mechanics, real decisions, real consequences. 19 quant engines, 88+ tradeable assets, corporate empire mechanics, 60+ ECFL-aligned courses. Gamification targeting 60%+ completion vs. 5–15% MOOC average.

Observe

Every user decision becomes a datapoint. Risk tolerance, sector biases, panic patterns, learning curves. At scale, this creates a structured behavioural finance dataset that doesn't exist anywhere else.

Monetise

Anonymised data licensed to banks, fintechs, insurers, and academia. Users get free Investment Portraits. The platform gets a data moat that deepens with every trade. Multiple revenue streams from one platform.

Professionalise

The same engines that simulate markets power professional analytics (Apollo), institutional training (Daedalus), and geopolitical risk intelligence (Ares). One engine layer, five subsidiaries.

3. Why This Exists — Three Broken Markets
Three problems. One shared root cause: nobody builds tools for financial understanding at scale.

Problem 1: Financial Literacy Crisis

64%
EU Adults Financially Illiterate

OECD PISA 2022. Young adults worst-affected.

5%
MOOC Completion Rate

Traditional education doesn't work. Lectures don't build risk intuition.

Problem 2: The Behavioural Data Gap

Nobody knows how young people actually make financial decisions. Banks use 5-question questionnaires. No large-scale, structured dataset exists on how 18–30 year olds make investment decisions under realistic conditions.

Problem 3: Institutional Training Is Broken

Universities use spreadsheets. Banks spend $24K/user/year on Bloomberg training. Current market simulators run server-side at ~50ms/tick. Daedalus runs browser-native at <2ms/tick — 25x faster.

ECFL — The Certification Moat NEW

The European Common Framework for Financial Literacy (ECFL) is a proprietary standard published by Quadratic Group, modelled on CEFR for languages. Already integrated into Aegis Empire with 60+ courses and exams.

F1 → F6
Six Progressive Bands

From elementary awareness to institutional authority. 100 discrete levels within bands.

4 Areas
EU/OECD Competence Areas

Money & Transactions, Planning & Managing, Risk & Reward, Financial Landscape.

v1.0
Published Standard

Independent advisory board, triennial review cycle, version-controlled.

The CEFR playbook: If universities adopt ECFL as a financial literacy standard, they need Quadratic's platform to certify students against it. ECFL certification on student CVs creates organic demand. 3–5 year play, but the standard is live and integrated now.
